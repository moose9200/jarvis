# Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-05-20

This policy lists what you cannot do with JARVIS. It's part of the Terms of Service. Breaking these rules can get your account suspended or terminated. Repeat or severe breaches will be reported to authorities.

---

## 1. No illegal use

You may not use JARVIS to plan, attempt, or commit any act that is illegal under Indian law, the law of your jurisdiction, or any jurisdiction where the affected parties live. This includes (non-exhaustive):

- Fraud, theft, money laundering, tax evasion.
- Stalking, doxxing, identity theft.
- Trafficking in humans, drugs, or weapons.
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — zero tolerance, immediate reporting to NCMEC/equivalent.
- Terrorism, incitement to violence, organised crime.

---

## 2. No abuse of connected accounts

When you connect Gmail / Outlook / Slack / Teams / WhatsApp / Linear / Jira / Notion / GitHub / Shopify / Freshdesk, the underlying terms of those services still bind you. You may not use JARVIS to:

- **Send spam, phishing, or unsolicited bulk messages** — even one-off mass campaigns. Use Mailchimp or a real ESP.
- Mass-send messages above the rate limits of the underlying provider.
- Violate Gmail Bulk Sender Guidelines, Microsoft Acceptable Use, Slack ToS, etc.
- Scrape or harvest data from accounts you don't own.
- Pretend a JARVIS-drafted message is exclusively human-authored where disclosure is required (some governments require AI labelling on advertising, journalism, academic work).

---

## 3. No abuse of our AI providers

You may not:

- Use JARVIS as a proxy to resell raw AI inference to others. Each user must have their own JARVIS account.
- Try to extract our system prompts or model weights.
- Run prompt-injection or jailbreak attacks against the AI tools to bypass safety guardrails.
- Generate content that violates the AI provider's content policy (Anthropic / OpenAI / Groq / Mistral / Google each publish their own — they all prohibit CSAM, instructions for weapons of mass destruction, etc., and ours mirrors theirs).
- Generate deepfakes of real people without their consent.

If you BYOAK, you remain responsible for compliance with that provider's terms — but you also remain responsible to us under this policy.

---

## 4. No abuse of our infrastructure

You may not:

- Run automated load against the API beyond documented rate limits (currently 5 register/login per minute per IP).
- Operate a bot, scraper, or scheduled tool against the API without using our official scheduled-task features (Intel Briefs, Decision Inbox sync).
- Conduct penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, or red-team activity against our servers without **prior written permission** from hemant@wbj.team.
- Bypass authentication, rate limiting, billing, or any other technical control.
- Disrupt the Service or other users (DoS, resource exhaustion, abuse-of-feature).

---

## 5. No abusive content

You may not use JARVIS to generate, store, or distribute:

- Harassment, threats, hate speech, or content that incites violence against a person or group.
- Sexual content involving minors. None. Ever.
- Non-consensual intimate imagery (revenge porn).
- Content designed to bully, defame, or impersonate a real person.
- Self-harm encouragement.

If you encounter content like this in your own account that's not yours (e.g. a malicious email), JARVIS itself is not the problem — but we'd appreciate a heads-up so we can improve our content filters.

---

## 6. No reverse-engineering or sublicensing

You may not:

- Decompile, disassemble, or reverse-engineer JARVIS.
- Frame, mirror, or rebrand JARVIS as your own service.
- Sublicense or transfer access to your account.
- Build a competing service using JARVIS as a backbone.

This does not prevent legitimate API integration with your own apps once we publish a Platform API (planned for a later release).

---

## 7. Disclosure obligations

Some jurisdictions require disclosure when AI is used in:

- Political advertising (EU, US states, India).
- Journalism (some outlets require disclosure for AI-assisted reporting).
- Academic submissions (many universities have specific policies).
- Marketing and product reviews (FTC guidance).

It is **your** responsibility to comply with disclosure requirements in your jurisdiction. We provide AI; you choose how to use the output.

---

## 8. Reporting abuse

If you see another user abusing JARVIS, or a security issue, please tell us:

- **General abuse:** hemant@wbj.team (a dedicated `abuse@` mailbox on the JARVIS domain will replace this once the production domain ships)
- **Security vulnerabilities:** hemant@wbj.team with subject "SECURITY" — please give us 90 days before public disclosure.
- **CSAM / urgent safety:** mark subject "URGENT". We will respond within 24 hours.

We do not retaliate against good-faith reporters.

---

## 9. Enforcement

If we believe you have breached this policy, we may at our discretion:

1. **Warning** — first-time minor breach. Email + 7 days to fix.
2. **Feature suspension** — disable specific features (e.g. email sending) while we investigate.
3. **Account suspension** — pause your account. We will try to give 7 days notice unless the breach is severe.
4. **Account termination** — permanent. Data deleted per `PRIVACY_POLICY.md` §7.
5. **Legal action / law enforcement referral** — for illegal activity, CSAM, fraud, or where required by law.

We may skip earlier steps for severe breaches (illegal use, security incidents, CSAM, repeat offences).
